Opening a Bank Account in Korea

Essential step for long-term residents. Major banks include KB, Shinhan, Woori, and Hana Bank.

Required Documents

You'll need: Passport, Alien Registration Card (ARC), phone number, and initial deposit (usually 10,000-50,000 KRW).

Choosing Your Bank

Consider location convenience, English support, online banking features, and international transfer fees.

Mobile Banking Setup

Download your bank's app and set up mobile banking for convenient 24/7 access to your account.

Using ATMs and Cards

Korean ATMs operate 24/7. Your debit card works at most locations, but always carry some cash.

Finding Housing in Korea

From goshiwons to apartments, understand your housing options in Korea.

Types of Housing

Goshiwon (small rooms), officetel (studio apartments), apartments, and sharehouses each offer different benefits.

Deposit System

Understand Jeonse (large deposit, no monthly rent) vs Wolse (smaller deposit + monthly rent) systems.

Finding Properties

Use apps like Zigbang, Dabang, or visit local real estate offices (부동산) in your desired area.

Moving In

Complete utility setup, register your address, and don't forget to get renter's insurance.

Healthcare in Korea

Korea has excellent healthcare with National Health Insurance covering most residents.

National Health Insurance

All residents must enroll in NHI. Coverage includes most medical treatments with affordable copays.

Visiting a Doctor

For minor issues, visit local clinics. For serious conditions, go to hospitals. Emergency rooms are available 24/7.

Pharmacies and Medication

Pharmacies (약국) are everywhere. Many medications available over-counter, but some require prescriptions.

Emergency Services

Call 119 for emergencies. Major hospitals have English-speaking staff and international patient services.
